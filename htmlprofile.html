About the Chipmunks

Who are they?

Based in Hollywood, CA, Alvin and the Chipmunks is a music group featuring three chipmunk brothers known for their high-pitched voices and energetic personalities. The group gained popularity through hit novelty records, TV shows, and movies. Over the decades, they’ve remained a beloved part of pop culture, entertaining multiple generations with their comedic adventures and musical covers.

Early life and Career

Alvin, Simon, and Theodore are chipmunk brothers who were discovered and adopted by songwriter Dave Seville, who also became their manager. Created by Ross Bagdasarian Sr., the Chipmunks made their debut in 1958 with the hit single “The Chipmunk Song (Christmas Don’t Be Late),” which launched their music career and became a holiday classic. Their early success led to albums, television appearances, and a growing fan base.
